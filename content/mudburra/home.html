Mudburra is a language of the Northern Territory (Australia). Many Mudburra people live in Elliott, Marlinja, Yarralin and Kalkaringi. The Mudburra e-Dictionary contains Mudburra words with English translations, illustrations, audio and detailed encyclopaedic information about plants, animals and cultural practices. Also included is an English index.

Every effort has been made to ensure that the information contained in this dictionary is completely public. No reference is made to men and women's business or private body parts. Nonetheless users are warned that there may be words and descriptions which may be culturally sensitive and which might not normally be used in certain public or community contexts. All texts, images and audio of deceased people in this database are used with permission from Mudburra families. Nonetheless users of this dictionary should be aware that, for Mudburra people, seeing images of deceased persons or hearing them may cause sadness or distress and in some cases, offend against strongly held cultural prohibitions.

Mudburra words and sentences come from Pharlap Dilkbarri Dixon†, Jumbo Kijilikarri Collins†, Sue ‘Lady’ Mangkanjangiwarra Dixon†,Lucy Hughes†, Albert Lalka Crowson†, Shannon Kulngankarri Dixon, Janey Walanyku Lunjabirni Dixon, Ray Dimakarri Dixon, Wendy Hughes, Maureen Bill, Susan Kingston, Raylene Bill, Bernie Dixon, Jeffrey Manawurda Dixon and Todman Dixon. They were compiled by Rebecca Green, Jennifer Green, Amanda Hamilton-Hollaway, Felicity Meakins, David Osgarby and Rob Pensalfini with additional contributions from Glenn Wightman, Patrick McConvell, David Nash and Mary Laughren. Headwords are spoken by Janey Dixon, Eleanor Dixon and Ethan Godfrey. Audio for sentences comes from Shannon Dixon, Ray Dixon, Albert Crowson, Janey Dixon and Wendy Hughes. The e-dictionary format was created by Caroline Jones and Jesse Tran with additional help from Marcel Reverter-Rambaldi, Lowana Tudor-Smith and Paul Williams.

It is a dictionary of use of this database that users ensure that any use of the information contained in this catalogue is consistent with the views and sensitivities of Mudburra people.

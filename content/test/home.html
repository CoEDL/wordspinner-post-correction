Bilinarra is a language of the Victoria River District of northern Australia. The Bilinarra e-Dictionary contains Bilinarra words with English translations, illustrations and detailed encyclopaedic information about plants, animals and cultural practices. Also included is a guide to the grammar of Bilinarra and an English index. This volume is ideal for both beginners and advanced speakers of Bilinarra, for translators and interpreters, and for anyone interested in learning more about Bilinarra language and culture.

The Bilinarra e-Dictionary was compiled by Felicity Meakins based on the the knowledge of Bilinarra language custodians Anzac Mungayari Mungganyi Jangari†, Hector Waitbiari Wanayari Jangari†, Ivy Kulngari Pumpat Nambijina-Nangari†, Molly Nyurruwangali Nambijina†, Dorothy Wumburr Nawurla†, Kathleen Juduwurr Nangala†, Doris Warnmal Namija†, Rook Julkiyarri Jurlama†, Barbara Warrmuya Bobby Nanagu, Mildred Milmarriya Gumingga Hector Nanagu and Sheila Marrbingali Hector Nanagu with additional linguistic contributions from Rachel Nordlinger, Lauren Campbell, Patrick McConvell, Justin Spence and Caroline Jones with scientific contributions from Glenn Wightman. Headwords are spoken by Sheila Hector and Cedrina Algy. Sentence audio comes from Old Anzac, Hector, Ivy Hector, Mildred Hector and Barbara Bobby. The e-dictionary format was created by Caroline Jones and Jesse Tran with additional help from Marcel Reverter-Rambaldi, Lowana Tudor-Smith and Paul Williams.

Every effort has been made to ensure that the information contained in this dictionary is completely public. No reference is made to men and women's business or private body parts. Nonetheless users are warned that there may be words and descriptions which may be culturally sensitive and which might not normally be used in certain public or community contexts. All texts, images and audio of deceased people in this database are used with permission from Bilinarra families. Nonetheless users of this catalogue should be aware that, for Bilinarra people, seeing images of deceased persons or hearing them may cause sadness or distress and in some cases, offend against strongly held cultural prohibitions.

It is a condition of use of this database that users ensure that any use of the information contained in this catalogue is consistent with the views and sensitivities of Bilinarra people.

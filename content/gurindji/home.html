Gurindji is a language of the southern Victoria River District and northern Tanami region in the Northern Territory (Australia). Gurindji people became well known in the 1960–70s due to their influence on Australian politics and the Indigenous rights movement. They were instrumental in gaining equal wages for Aboriginal cattle station employees and they were also the first Aboriginal group to recover control of their traditional lands.

The Gurindji e-Dictionary contains Gurindji words with English translations, illustrations, audio and detailed encyclopaedic information about plants, animals and cultural practices. Also included is an English index. This volume is ideal for both beginners and advanced speakers of Gurindji, for translators and interpreters, and for anyone interested in learning more about Gurindji language and culture.

Every effort has been made to ensure that the information contained in this dictionary is completely public. No reference is made to men and women's business or private body parts. Nonetheless users are warned that there may be words and descriptions which may be culturally sensitive and which might not normally be used in certain public or community contexts. All texts, images and audio of deceased people in this database are used with permission from Gurindji families. Nonetheless users of this catalogue should be aware that, for Gurindji people, seeing images of deceased persons or hearing them may cause sadness or distress and in some cases, offend against strongly held cultural prohibitions.

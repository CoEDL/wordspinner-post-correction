Ngarinyman is a language of the northern Victoria River District of the Northern Territory (Australia). Many Ngarinyman people live in Yarralin, Bulla Camp, Amanbidji (Kildurk) and around Timber Creek. The Ngarinyman e-Dictionary contains Ngarinyman words with English translations, illustrations, audio and detailed encyclopaedic information about plants, animals and cultural practices. Also included is an English index.

Every effort has been made to ensure that the information contained in this dictionary is completely public. No reference is made to men and women's business or private body parts. Nonetheless users are warned that there may be words and descriptions which may be culturally sensitive and which might not normally be used in certain public or community contexts. All texts, images and audio of deceased people in this database are used with permission from Ngarinyman families. Nonetheless users of this dictionary should be aware that, for Ngarinyman people, seeing images of deceased persons or hearing them may cause sadness or distress and in some cases, offend against strongly held cultural prohibitions.

Ngarinyman words and sentences come from Ronny Balwanjer†, Joy Campbell, Christine Daly, Daisy Harrington†, Nina Humbert†, Kathleen Juduwurr†, Snowy Kulmilya†, Jessie Kinyayi, Sharon Macmillan, Noeline Nemit, Roy Nuwalad Harrington, Annie Packsaddle†, Nellie Packsaddle†, Lucy Pedwell, Eileen Roberts†, Josie Roberts, Laurie Roberts, Nancy Roberts, Mikayla Friday-Shaw, Elsie Snowy, Peter Waterloo†, Bobby Widijburru† and Maggie Wilinkari†. The dictionary was compiled by Caroline Jones, Eva Schultze-Berndt, Jessica Denniss and Felicity Meakins with additional contributions from John Laurence, Patrick McConvell, Colleen Moerkerken, Candide Simard and Glenn Wightman. The e-dictionary format was created by Caroline Jones and Jesse Tran with additional help from Marcel Reverter-Rambaldi, Lowana Tudor-Smith and Paul Williams.

It is a dictionary of use of this database that users ensure that any use of the information contained in this catalogue is consistent with the views and sensitivities of Ngarinyman people.
